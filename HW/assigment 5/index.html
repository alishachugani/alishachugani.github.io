PANAMA

Panama is located in Central America and it is bordered by Costa Rica to the west, Colombia to the east, the Caribbean Ocean to the north and the Pacific Ocean to the south. Panama is an “S” shape isthmus with a population of 3,608,431 according to the 2014 census. Panama’s official currency is the balboa and the United States dollar, and its official language is Spanish. Panama is made up ten provinces and five indigenous regions. Panama has a tropical climate and its official bird if the Harpy Eagle. Panama’s current president is Juan Carlos Varela and the vice president is Isabel Saint Malo.

    Bocas Del Toro

    Bocas del Toro is one of Panama’s provinces. It is next to the border to Costa Rica and is one of the most visited places. Bocas is a very relax place in which people go to chill, party and surf. Different small islands compose it. During a time a year one can see dolphins and different turtles.

    Canal de Panamá

    Panama is best known for the Panama Canal. The Panama Canal is a 50-mile, man-made canal cutting through the Isthmus of Panama that connects the Pacific and Atlantic Oceans. It was constructed by the United States in order to find a waterway route between the two oceans. . Panama officially gained full control over the canal on December 31, 1999 and is one of the most influential incomes the country has.

    Casco Viejo

    Casco Anitguo is on of the most popular places in Panama. In Casco we can find everything, we have: hotels, the must beautiful church’s, restaurants, museums, rooftop bars, the presidential palace and old and new houses. Casco is one of the only places in which you can find people from all ages.

    San Blas

    San Blass is known in Panama because it has the most beautiful tiny islands in the entire country. The area known as San Blass is under the control of one of the indigenous regions, and because of that there are no man made buildings in the area. This allows a person to connect better with nature and appreciate the view; it is a good way to get away from society considering that there is no cell phone signal in the entire area.

    Volcan Barú

    Volcan Baru is the highest point in Panama. As the name says it, it is a volcano. Usually people do early hiking trips to the volcano because early in the morning, if the day is not cloudy, one can see both oceans. This is the only point in Panama in which one can see both oceans standing in one same place, it might be one of the few places on Earth in which that is possible.
